Database Normalization is the formal systemic approach of decomposing tables to eliminate data redundancy and undesirable characteristics like Insertion, Update, and Deletion Anomalies. First Normal Form (1NF) states that all attribute domains must contain only atomic, indivisible values. Second Normal Form (2NF) mandates that the relation must already be in 1NF, and all non-prime attributes must be fully functionally dependent on the entire composite primary key, thereby eliminating partial dependencies entirely. Third Normal Form (3NF) advances this structure by verifying it holds 2NF compliance and ensuring that no non-prime attributes exhibit transitive dependencies on the primary identifier mechanism.
